Are transit networks strong-link or weak-link systems?

22.12.2016 16:33
It’s a question as old as sports itself: can you go farther with a few standout players, or a deep bench of pretty good players? In Malcolm Gladwell’s Revisionist History podcast, he argues that it depends on the sport, and frames the discussion by introducing a dichotomy between what he calls “strong-link” and “weak-link” systems. The capability of a strong-link system is dominated by that of the strongest link; for example, Gladwell argues that basketball teams are strong-link systems because their performance is dominated by that of the best player on the team; when you have a Steph Curry or Michael Jordan putting up 25-30 points every night, that’s a hard problem for any amount of teamwork by the opposition to overcome.
